# LinkedIn Radar Review Routine

A recurring review routine for maintaining a LinkedIn-based trend radar, improving source quality, reviewing signal maturity and deciding follow-up actions.

## Purpose

This document defines how teams, organizations, agencies and AI-assisted workflows can maintain a LinkedIn Trend Radar over time.

The radar is not a one-time source list or collection of interesting posts.

It is an operating routine.

A review routine helps ensure that:

* source mix remains aligned with the radar purpose
* feed noise is reduced over time
* observations are reviewed before becoming trend notes
* signals are not over-weighted too early
* evidence gaps are visible
* governance, adoption and content handoffs are tracked
* outdated records are archived or deleted
* AI-assisted steps remain limited to minimized and approved inputs

The review routine turns the radar from a collection habit into a repeatable intelligence workflow.

## Core idea

A LinkedIn Trend Radar needs cadence.

Without cadence, observations accumulate without interpretation.

The review routine connects:

```txt id="b48xjy"
source discovery
→ feed review
→ signal detection
→ trend notes
→ evidence tasks
→ handoffs
→ retention decisions
→ source mix adjustment
```

The routine should be scaled to the organization’s purpose, risk level, topic volatility and available capacity.

## What this routine is

This routine is:

* a maintenance cadence for the LinkedIn Trend Radar
* a signal review process
* a source quality review process
* a noise reduction mechanism
* an evidence triage process
* a handoff decision routine
* a retention and cleanup routine
* a reusable operating model for teams and AI-assisted workflows

## What this routine is not

This routine is not:

* a scraping schedule
* a requirement to monitor LinkedIn continuously
* a social media publishing calendar
* a replacement for evidence review
* a replacement for legal, privacy or governance review
* a requirement to store all observed links or posts
* a productivity ritual without decision value

The routine should exist only where the radar purpose justifies the review effort.

## Review cadence overview

The review routine can operate at four levels.

| Cadence                                | Purpose                                                                                | Typical duration |
| -------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------: |
| Weekly radar review                    | Capture observations, classify early signals, identify immediate actions               |    30–60 minutes |
| Monthly synthesis review               | Identify repeated signals, create trend notes, review evidence gaps                    |   60–120 minutes |
| Quarterly source and governance review | Review source mix, noise, retention, access and radar purpose                          |        2–4 hours |
| Event-triggered review                 | Respond to major regulation, incident, platform change, product shift or public debate |        as needed |

The cadence should be adjusted to the topic area.

Fast-moving topics such as AI security or regulation may require more frequent review than slower strategic themes.

## Review roles

A review routine may involve different roles depending on the implementation.

| Role                | Responsibility                                                                        |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Radar owner         | Maintains the routine and decides what moves forward                                  |
| Source reviewer     | Reviews source mix, source gaps and noise patterns                                    |
| Signal reviewer     | Classifies observations and maturity levels                                           |
| Evidence reviewer   | Checks whether external evidence is sufficient                                        |
| Governance reviewer | Reviews risk, compliance, privacy or accountability implications                      |
| Adoption reviewer   | Checks workflow, capability and operating-model relevance                             |
| Content reviewer    | Checks whether a signal should become a briefing, explainer or publication asset      |
| AI assistant        | Supports classification, summarization or routing using minimized and approved inputs |

One person may hold several roles in a small workflow.

In organization-ready implementations, roles should be explicit.

## Weekly radar review

The weekly review is designed to keep the radar active without overbuilding the process.

### Purpose

The weekly review captures recent observations, classifies early signals and decides immediate next actions.

### Suggested agenda

```txt id="kwhxz6"
1. Review radar purpose and active topic clusters
2. Review curated feed or saved observations
3. Capture minimal observations
4. Classify source archetype
5. Classify interest trigger
6. Classify signal type
7. Assign preliminary maturity level
8. Identify evidence needs
9. Decide next action
10. Update watchlist or trend-note backlog
```

### Weekly review questions

```txt id="tbjqft"
Which observations were captured this week?
Which source archetypes produced useful signals?
Which observations are likely noise?
Which signals appeared repeatedly?
Which signals came mainly from vendors?
Which signals may indicate operational pressure?
Which signals need external evidence?
Which signals should remain on watchlist?
Which observations should be discarded?
```

### Weekly outputs

| Finding                      | Output                     |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Isolated but relevant signal | watchlist entry            |
| Repeated signal              | trend note candidate       |
| Unsupported claim            | external evidence task     |
| Vendor-heavy topic           | vendor evaluation question |
| Risk-heavy signal            | governance question        |
| Adoption-relevant signal     | adoption question          |
| Strong framing               | content idea               |
| Low-value input              | discard or archive         |
| Source imbalance             | source map update          |

## Monthly synthesis review

The monthly review identifies patterns across weekly observations.

### Purpose

The monthly review decides which observations deserve deeper treatment.

### Suggested agenda

```txt id="lbq0rn"
1. Review weekly observations
2. Cluster similar signals
3. Check source diversity
4. Check maturity movement
5. Review external evidence
6. Convert selected signals into trend notes
7. Route signals to governance, adoption, vendor evaluation or content workflows
8. Update source map and noise notes
9. Close or archive low-value records
```

### Monthly review questions

```txt id="o2244n"
Which signals repeated across multiple weeks?
Which signals appeared across distinct source archetypes?
Which topics moved in maturity?
Which topics remained vendor-heavy?
Which topics gained practitioner evidence?
Which signals now require governance review?
Which signals now require adoption review?
Which signals are ready for content planning?
Which signals should be archived or deleted?
```

### Monthly outputs

| Pattern                               | Output                                     |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Repeated signal with source diversity | full trend note                            |
| Vendor narrative without evidence     | vendor evaluation question + evidence task |
| Emerging operational pressure         | adoption question                          |
| Emerging accountability issue         | governance question                        |
| Strong public framing                 | content idea                               |
| Poor source diversity                 | source discovery task                      |
| High feed noise                       | feed-noise review action                   |
| Outdated observation                  | archive or delete                          |

## Quarterly source and governance review

The quarterly review checks whether the radar still works as an operating system.

### Purpose

The quarterly review evaluates source quality, source diversity, privacy controls, retention logic and continued relevance of the radar purpose.

### Suggested agenda

```txt id="ws5eeq"
1. Review radar purpose
2. Review source map
3. Review source diversity
4. Review noise patterns
5. Review evidence quality
6. Review handoff quality
7. Review access and retention status
8. Review AI-assisted processing rules
9. Update source discovery priorities
10. Adjust cadence or scope
```

### Quarterly review questions

```txt id="8z7js7"
Does the radar purpose still match organizational needs?
Are topic clusters still relevant?
Which source archetypes produced useful signals?
Which source archetypes produced mostly noise?
Which perspectives are missing?
Are vendor voices overrepresented?
Are technical, governance and practitioner sources balanced?
Are external evidence checks sufficient?
Are working records minimized?
Are access levels still appropriate?
Are retention statuses up to date?
Are AI-assisted steps limited to approved inputs?
Which records should be archived or deleted?
```

### Quarterly outputs

| Review result          | Output                                                                       |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Radar purpose changed  | update topic clusters and source map                                         |
| Source imbalance found | source discovery task                                                        |
| Noise increased        | feed-noise reduction action                                                  |
| Evidence quality weak  | external evidence process update                                             |
| Handoffs unclear       | revise next-action categories                                                |
| Records over-retained  | archive or delete                                                            |
| Access too broad       | restrict working layer                                                       |
| AI use unclear         | update AI-assisted use rules                                                 |
| Mature topic           | move to trend dossier, governance toolkit, adoption work or content planning |

## Event-triggered review

An event-triggered review happens when a topic changes quickly.

Triggers may include:

* major regulation
* security incident
* platform policy change
* major AI model release
* high-profile lawsuit
* major vendor announcement
* public-sector guidance
* industry report
* viral public controversy
* internal leadership escalation
* procurement pressure
* employee concern or adoption spike

### Event-triggered review questions

```txt id="5tr0wz"
What changed?
Which active signals are affected?
Does the maturity level change?
Is external evidence available?
Does this create governance urgency?
Does this create adoption pressure?
Does this affect existing content or messaging?
Does the source map need new source archetypes?
Does this require legal, privacy, security or leadership review?
```

### Event-triggered outputs

| Trigger                 | Possible output                        |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Regulation              | governance question, policy review     |
| Security incident       | security review, evidence task         |
| Vendor announcement     | vendor evaluation question             |
| Model release           | trend note, adoption question          |
| Public controversy      | reputation review, content question    |
| Internal escalation     | leadership briefing, governance review |
| Employee adoption spike | adoption operating-model question      |

## Review decision categories

Each reviewed record should receive a decision.

| Decision                       | Meaning                                              |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Discard                        | Not relevant or not useful                           |
| Watch                          | Potentially relevant, but too early                  |
| Keep                           | Relevant and proportionate to retain                 |
| Convert to trend note          | Sufficient signal structure exists                   |
| Add evidence task              | Claims need external verification                    |
| Add governance question        | Risk, accountability or compliance relevance exists  |
| Add adoption question          | Workflow, capability or role relevance exists        |
| Add vendor evaluation question | Market claim or procurement relevance exists         |
| Add content idea               | Communication or explanation opportunity exists      |
| Escalate                       | Legal, privacy, security or leadership review needed |
| Archive                        | No longer active but useful for historical context   |
| Delete                         | No longer needed or not proportionate to retain      |

Decisions should include a short rationale where the record remains active.

## Retention review

The radar should not retain records indefinitely by default.

Retention should be based on necessity, proportionality and future use.

Recommended retention statuses:

| Status  | Meaning                                   |
| ------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Keep    | Active and relevant                       |
| Review  | Usefulness or necessity is unclear        |
| Archive | Not active, but retained with reason      |
| Delete  | Not needed or not proportionate to retain |

Retention questions:

```txt id="f2dsmc"
Is this record still needed?
Does it support an active radar purpose?
Does it include identifiable references?
Can the record be minimized?
Can the record be converted to an aggregate insight?
Should the source reference be removed?
Should the record be archived?
Should the record be deleted?
```

If a record includes identifiable data, retention review should be stricter.

## Access review

Access level should match the sensitivity and purpose of the record.

Recommended access levels:

| Access level                    | Use                                                            |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Individual working note         | Personal or role-based early observation                       |
| Team working note               | Shared with a defined team                                     |
| Controlled internal record      | Restricted due to identifiable references, risk or sensitivity |
| Governance review record        | Used for legal, privacy, security or accountability review     |
| Shareable fictionalized example | Safe for training or method explanation                        |
| External publication candidate  | Requires evidence and content review before publication        |

Access review questions:

```txt id="07jcpy"
Who needs access?
What is the purpose of access?
Does the record contain identifiable references?
Can the record be minimized before sharing?
Does the record need governance review?
Is external publication being considered?
```

## Evidence review

Evidence review determines whether a signal can move forward.

Recommended evidence statuses:

```txt id="m9ss0a"
none
anecdotal only
partial
external evidence available
sufficient for trend note
sufficient for governance review
sufficient for content review
needs expert review
```

Evidence review questions:

```txt id="5bt5vl"
Is the signal based only on LinkedIn observations?
Are external sources available?
Are sources primary or secondary?
Is the evidence current?
Is the evidence relevant to the organizational context?
Is the evidence sufficient for the proposed next action?
Does the evidence reduce or increase urgency?
```

Evidence thresholds should increase with decision impact.

A watchlist entry needs less evidence than a governance recommendation or external publication.

## Source map review

The radar routine should regularly update the source map.

Review:

```txt id="zdn9km"
Which source archetypes produced useful signals?
Which source archetypes produced mostly noise?
Which topic clusters need new source types?
Which sources need lower weighting?
Which source categories should be archived?
Which source categories need external evidence support?
Which source types are missing from the current radar?
```

Actions:

```txt id="45vx56"
add source archetype
reduce source weight
add counter-source
archive source category
update noise risk
update best use
change review cadence
```

## Handoff review

A signal should be routed when its next action is clear enough.

| Handoff target             | When to use                                                      |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Trend radar backlog        | Signal needs further monitoring                                  |
| External evidence layer    | Signal requires verification                                     |
| Governance workflow        | Risk, accountability, privacy, vendor or compliance implications |
| Adoption workflow          | Workflow, role, capability or operating-model implications       |
| Content workflow           | Explanation, briefing, campaign, FAQ or publication opportunity  |
| Vendor evaluation workflow | Tool claim, procurement pressure or vendor category signal       |
| Leadership briefing        | Topic creates strategic or organizational urgency                |
| Archive                    | Signal no longer active but historically useful                  |
| Delete                     | Record no longer necessary or proportionate                      |

Handoffs should include enough context for the receiving workflow without copying unnecessary source data.

## AI-assisted use

An AI assistant may help classify source archetypes, signal types, maturity levels and next actions if the input has already been minimized and approved.

The AI assistant should not request, infer or store unnecessary personal data.

The AI assistant should work from source archetypes, minimal signal notes and external evidence whenever possible.

Useful AI-assisted tasks include:

* clustering minimized observations
* suggesting signal types
* suggesting maturity levels
* identifying repeated patterns
* drafting review summaries
* suggesting evidence tasks
* proposing handoff categories
* identifying records that may need retention review
* drafting monthly or quarterly review summaries

Avoid using AI for:

* extracting LinkedIn posts at scale
* processing raw connection exports without review
* profiling named individuals
* ranking people by influence or trustworthiness
* inferring sensitive attributes
* storing unnecessary identifiers
* replacing human review for governance-sensitive signals

The review routine keeps the LinkedIn Trend Radar operational by turning observations into decisions.

Each review cycle should clarify what to keep, what to investigate, what to route, what to archive and what to delete.
